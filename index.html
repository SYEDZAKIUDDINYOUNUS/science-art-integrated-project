WHAT ARE CYCLONES

Cyclone is a system of low atmospheric pressure in which the barometric is steep.

















CYCLONES: TYPES AND MECHANISM

Cyclones represent circular fluid motion rotating in the same direction as the Earth. This means that the inward spiralling winds in a cyclone rotate anticlockwise in the Northern Hemisphere and clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere of the Earth.










BASIC DIFFERENCE BETWEEN TROPICAL CYCLONE AND EXTRA-TROPICAL CYCLONE
The term “tropical cyclone” is used to refer to warm-core, low-pressure systems that develop over tropical or subtropical oceans.
